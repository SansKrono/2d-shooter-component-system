# Dual-Currency Economy Design: Data vs Essence

## Overview

Create a **strategic economy** around two currencies — **DATA (tech)** and **ESSENCE (magic)** — that forces meaningful spending choices and reinforces build identity. Players must decide which economy to invest in, making currency spending a form of character building, not just a menu transaction.

---

## Core Design Principle

**Abundance creates strategy. Scarcity creates desperation.**

Instead of one currency that players hoarde or waste, two currencies means:
- High-resource moments: "Should I spend this on defense or offense?"
- Low-resource moments: "Which currency do I chase for this floor?"
- Build definition: "I'm a Data player" vs "I'm an Essence player" vs "I adapt"

---

## Currency 1: DATA (Tech Currency)

**Metaphor:** Computational power, information, hacking codes

**Appearance:** Blue/cyan, digital, streams of bits, glowing runes

**How you earn it:**
- Destroy tech enemies: +3-5 Data
- Complete hacking challenges (optional rooms): +10-20 Data
- Precision shots (accuracy > 80%): +1 Data per shot
- Destroy tech infrastructure (servers, robots): +7-15 Data
- Tech-mode kills (shooting in tech mode): +1 multiplier

**Rate:** Medium/reliable (5-15 per enemy)

**Key property:** Abundant and repeatable. Players who specialize in tech get a constant income.

---

## Currency 2: ESSENCE (Magic Currency)

**Metaphor:** Life force, magical corruption, dimensional energy

**Appearance:** Purple/pink, swirling, chaotic, corrupted auras

**How you earn it:**
- Destroy corrupted enemies: +5-8 Essence
- Kill enemies while in corruption zones: +2 Essence per kill
- Overkill corrupted enemies (burst damage): +10-20 Essence
- Ritual kills (defeat a boss without taking damage): +50 Essence
- Magic-mode chain kills (3+ enemies in 5 seconds): +5 Essence per kill

**Rate:** Volatile/spike-based (0-20 per encounter, but rare 50+ moments)

**Key property:** Rare but rewarding. Players who hunt for corruption get moments of wealth.

---

## The Strategic Tension

### Tech Player
- Earns Data steadily by playing accurately
- Can afford consistent upgrades every 1-2 floors
- Build identity: Reliable, precise, high control
- Risk: If you get behind, recovery is slow (consistency takes time)
- Strength: Always has some currency to spend

### Essence Player
- Earns Essence in bursts by seeking corruption and high-risk encounters
- Affords powerful upgrades rarely, but they're game-changing
- Build identity: Powerful, unpredictable, high reward
- Risk: Long dry spells with no currency
- Strength: When you hit a spike, you get massive power

### Hybrid Player
- Earns both but not as much as specialists
- Can adapt: stuck on a tech boss? Spend Data on upgrades. Chaos floor? Hunt for Essence
- Build identity: Flexible, responsive, adaptive
- Risk: Jack-of-all-trades, master-of-none without planning
- Strength: Survive anything with the right tool

## Shops & Spending

### Tech Shops (Data currency)

**Tier 1 (5-15 Data each)**
- Precision Module: +15% accuracy
- Overclock Shard: +20% fire rate
- Data Core: +10% damage

**Tier 2 (25-40 Data)**
- Firewall Augment: temporary shield (recharges every 10s)
- Hacking Exploit: reveal secret room on this floor
- Processing Array: pierce through 2 extra enemies

**Tier 3 (50-80 Data)**
- CPU Overload: double fire rate for 10s (recharge: 20s)
- Quantum Redirect: bullets bounce off walls (lasts until floor end)
- Encryption Rune: enemies take 30% longer to react

**Tier 4 (100+ Data)**
- Singularity: Massive damage explosion on reload
- Meta-Processor: unlock a synergy you're close to

**Shop behavior:** Tech shops ALWAYS have stock. You can always buy (if you have currency). Reliable vendor = reliable progression.

### Magic Shops (Essence currency)

**Tier 1 (5-12 Essence)**
- Corruption Vein: attacks leave corruption trails
- Chaos Bolt: projectiles randomize direction (+20% damage, -30% accuracy)
- Essence Infusion: next boss kill grants +25 Essence

**Tier 2 (20-35 Essence)**
- Void Touch: Void enemies drop 2x Essence
- Ritual Catalyst: Ritual kills grant doubled Essence
- Spreading Curse: Corruption spreads 2x faster

**Tier 3 (50-75 Essence)**
- Aetheric Convergence: Merge two shots into one massive corruptive beam
- Corruption Mastery: Enemies in corruption zones drop Essence on hit
- Dimension Breach: Summon corrupted creature that fights for you (15s)

**Tier 4 (120+ Essence)**
- Void Ascension: Become invulnerable while standing in corruption, damage increases 200%
- Equilibrium Break: swap your remaining Data and Essence (gamble mechanic)

**Shop behavior:** Magic shops have LIMITED STOCK or may not appear every floor. Scarcity = urgency. If you see an Essence shop with something you need, you *must* decide now.

## Economy Pacing

### Floor 1 (Tutorial)
- Player earns: 30-50 Data, 5-10 Essence
- Available shops: 1 Tech shop
- Message: "Tech currency is the safe path"

### Floor 2-3 (Early game)
- Earnings: 15-25 Data per floor, 3-8 Essence per floor
- Available shops: 1 Tech shop + occasional Magic shop
- First major decision: "Do I specialize or stay balanced?"

### Floor 4-5 (Mid game)
- Earnings: 20-40 Data per floor, 5-20 Essence per floor (more volatile)
- Available shops: Varies (maybe both, maybe neither)
- Pressure: "My build needs one more piece"

### Floor 6+ (Late game)
- Earnings: 30-60 Data per floor, 10-40 Essence per floor
- Available shops: Rare, expensive, high-impact
- Message: "Currency matters less, synergies matter more"

## Economy Interactions

### Conversion Mechanics (Optional)

**NO direct 1:1 conversion.** Instead:

**Corruption Rituals (Magic → Data)**
- Spend 20 Essence to unlock a hacking challenge (earn +50 Data if completed)
- Converts risk (Essence) into reward (Data)

**Tech Overload (Data → Essence)**
- Spend 50 Data to overcharge a tech device, which corrupts and releases +30 Essence
- Converts stable (Data) into volatile (Essence)

These should be **rare** (once per floor max) and feel like meaningful sacrifices, not exploits.

## Shops as Story

### Tech Vendors
**Keeper of the Archive** (AI-like personality)
- "Excellent. You understand the value of precision."
- Sells upgrades confidently
- Appears every floor (reliable)
- Voice: Clinical, organized, efficient

### Magic Vendors
**The Broker** (mysterious, cult-like)
- "Ah, you sense it too... the corruption calls to those with will."
- Appears sporadically
- Voice: Cryptic, seductive, dangerous
- "I don't always have what you seek. You must earn it."

**Story implication:** Players who favor one currency get distinct NPC relationships. Hybrid players see both voices, creating narrative ambiguity.

---

## Starting Economy

**Basement floor grant:**
- All players start with 0 Data, 0 Essence (earn it, don't hoard)
- First enemy drops: 5 Data

This forces early decisions and prevents "save-scumming" a perfect early build.

---

## Win Conditions & Endgame Currency

### Beating a Boss
- Tech boss defeated: +20-40 Data
- Magic boss defeated: +30-50 Essence
- **Hybrid boss (requires both):** Player must have both currencies to even enter the arena

### Final Floor Shop
After defeating the final boss:
- Players can spend remaining currency on post-game unlocks
- Cosmetics, alternate skins, lore pieces
- Encourages different runs with different economic strategies

---

## Currency Perks & Milestones

### At 100 Data Spent (Lifetime)
- Unlock "Hacker" achievement
- Unlock blue cosmetic (blue glow, tech-aligned skins)
- Unlock "Data Cache" passive: earn +1 Data per 5 kills (permanent unlock)

### At 100 Essence Spent (Lifetime)
- Unlock "Corruptor" achievement
- Unlock purple cosmetic (corruption aura, chaos skins)
- Unlock "Void Resonance" passive: killed corrupted enemies grant +1 Essence (permanent unlock)

### At 150 of Each Spent
- Unlock "Balanced" achievement
- Unlock hybrid cosmetics
- Unlock "Equilibrium" passive: Data and Essence costs reduced by 10%

## Implementation Notes

### C_Currency Component
```gdscript
class_name C_Currency extends Component

var data: int = 0
var essence: int = 0
var lifetime_data_earned: int = 0
var lifetime_essence_earned: int = 0
var lifetime_data_spent: int = 0
var lifetime_essence_spent: int = 0

signal currency_changed(new_data: int, new_essence: int)
signal milestone_reached(type: String)  # "hacker", "corruptor", "balanced"

func add_currency(c_type: String, amount: int):
	if c_type == "data":
		data += amount
		lifetime_data_earned += amount
	elif c_type == "essence":
		essence += amount
		lifetime_essence_earned += amount
	currency_changed.emit(data, essence)
	check_milestones()

func spend_currency(c_type: String, amount: int) -> bool:
	if c_type == "data" and data >= amount:
		data -= amount
		lifetime_data_spent += amount
		currency_changed.emit(data, essence)
		return true
	elif c_type == "essence" and essence >= amount:
		essence -= amount
		lifetime_essence_spent += amount
		currency_changed.emit(data, essence)
		return true
	return false

func check_milestones():
	if lifetime_data_spent == 100:
		milestone_reached.emit("hacker")
	if lifetime_essence_spent == 100:
		milestone_reached.emit("corruptor")
	if lifetime_data_spent >= 150 and lifetime_essence_spent >= 150:
		milestone_reached.emit("balanced")
```

- Top-left corner: Two badges showing Data (blue) and Essence (purple)

### Shop System
```gdscript
class_name ShopVendor extends Node

@export var vendor_type: String  # "tech" or "magic"
@export var items: Array[ShopItem]
@export var always_appears: bool  # true for tech, false for magic

signal purchase_made(item: ShopItem)

func purchase(item: ShopItem, player: Entity) -> bool:
	var c_currency = player.get_component(C_Currency)
	if vendor_type == "tech":
		if c_currency.spend_currency("data", item.cost_data):
			apply_effect(item, player)
			purchase_made.emit(item)
			return true
	elif vendor_type == "magic":
		if c_currency.spend_currency("essence", item.cost_essence):
			apply_effect(item, player)
			purchase_made.emit(item)
			return true
	return false
```

## Balancing Framework

### Economic Health Check

Per floor, ask:

1. **Can a tech player afford an upgrade?** Yes = healthy
2. **Can a magic player afford an upgrade?** Sometimes = healthy tension
3. **Can a hybrid player afford both?** Rarely = forces choice = good

If all three are always yes: currency is too abundant.
If all three are rarely: currency is too scarce.
If any one is never: that path feels dead.

## Future Extensions (Not Phase 1)

- **Currency sinks:** Permanent upgrades you save for across runs (meta progression)
- **Gambling shops:** 5 Data → 1d6 Essence (risk/reward conversion)
- **Negative currency:** Debt mechanics ("You owe the Broker 50 Essence...")
- **Currency duels:** Boss fights where spending currency in real-time changes battle state
- **Faction currency:** Earn standing with Tech vendors or Magic vendors based on spending

## Testing Checklist

- [ ] Players earn ~20-40 Data per floor (reliable)
- [ ] Players earn ~0-20 Essence per floor (volatile, spiky)
- [ ] At least one shop available per 2 floors
- [ ] Tech shops always appear, magic shops are rare
- [ ] Cost scaling feels right (early items 5-15, mid 30-50, late 80+)
- [ ] A player can complete a run with pure tech, pure magic, or hybrid strategies
- [ ] No single currency is 10x more valuable than the other
- [ ] Players feel tension when deciding what to buy
- [ ] Achievement milestones hit around 40-60% of a playthrough
